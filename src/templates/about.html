About the project

A recommendation engine that reads the fic, not the tags

Opsis is the alpha of a larger project called Siagnos — a personal fanfiction taste engine. Where Siagnos will eventually model an individual reader's taste from their own reading history, Opsis answers a narrower, immediately useful question: given one fic, what else is close to it?

Why this exists

Tag search finds fics. It doesn't understand them.

AO3's own search is built on tags, and tags are inconsistent by nature — two readers tag the same fic differently, and plenty of relevant fics are undertagged or missed entirely. Kudos and bookmark counts measure popularity, not fit. A fic with ten thousand kudos can still miss what made you like the one you just read. Opsis compares each fic's summary directly, checks it against fandom and relationship overlap, and weighs in how well it's already resonated with readers — so what surfaces is close in substance, not just matching in label.

How a recommendation gets made

From a fic you name to a shelf of fics that fit

  1. 01

    Resolve

    You give a work ID, a link, or a title and author. Opsis checks whether that fic is already known.

  2. 02

    Fetch, if needed

    If the fic isn't in the archive yet, Opsis retrieves its metadata directly from AO3 and stores it.

  3. 03

    Represent

    The fic's summary and metadata are turned into a numeric representation — an embedding — that captures its content, not just its labels.

  4. 04

    Compare

    That representation is measured against every other fic in the archive using cosine similarity, a way of scoring how close two fics sit in content.

  5. 05

    Recommend

    The closest matches come back as a ranked shelf, each with its own similarity score.

On copyright and consent

The fic stays on AO3. Opsis only points to it.

Opsis stores metadata — titles, summaries, tags, and stats — not fic text, and every recommendation links back to the original work on AO3. Automated collection was discussed with the Organization for Transformative Works' Communications Committee in advance, with scope limited to metadata and rate limits respected throughout. Opsis is a discovery layer on top of AO3, not a copy of it.

What's next

Opsis is the alpha. Siagnos is the destination.

Opsis answers "what's similar to this fic." Siagnos, the larger project this grew out of, goes further — learning an individual reader's own taste from what they actually finish, drop, or return to, rather than comparing fics to each other in the abstract.
